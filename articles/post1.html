Plastic World Waste Threatened The Earth Existence, Who's At Fault?

Nowadays, there are quite a lot environmental problems in the world. One of them is anthropogenic pollution. Anthropogenic pollution is a very serious problem that has been discussed lately because it has been an unsolved problem since the first period of humans began to learn control the waste until now.


If we look back to the past, anthropogenic pollution was not a quite big issue compared to now. The amount of pollution began to increase after the Industrial Revolution when the population started to produce diverse products with chemical compounds as the main materials. These chemical-based products are still heavily produced until now, despite several researches and studies have stated that the products cause anthropogenic pollution. This kind of pollution is dangerous because it consists of toxic chemical that threaten both human and animal health and also the climate. Thus several countries began to take an action and united in a global partnership called as United Nation to put this issue as The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.


UN realized that to be able to avoid the worst possibility that might happened because of this issue, they need to come up with strategies while tackling the climate change issue and work together to preserve the ocean and rain-forest at the same time.

One of the solutions ─ waste management is in line with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) target, that in 2030 every country substantially reduces waste production through prevention, reduction, recycling, and reusing the waste, so it can guarantee sustainable production and consumption patterns.

How many years does it take for the nature to decompose plastic? 

One of the dangerous anthropogenic pollutions is a plastic waste. Plastic waste is difficult to decompose by nature. Normally, plastic items take up to 1000 years to decompose in landfills. The plastic bag we usually use in our everyday life takes 10-20 years to decompose, while plastic bottles take 450 years . The amount of plastic waste continues to increase year by year due to the increasing economic growth. The waste of the manufacturing industry is one of the biggest contributors, followed by household waste. According to the Indonesian Ministry of Industry in 2016, the amount of waste in Indonesia has reached 65.2 million tons per year. 


Plastic waste is not only affecting the land but also the water. Some people in several countries still have a bad habit to throw the garbage in the river. The stream that supposed to be clear becomes dirty. This reduces water quality. The garbage flow from the streams along the way to the sea. This cause the sea becomes dirty and the creatures inside get affected by them. 


Indonesia is one of the countries that still have this habit. The river water in several regions in Indonesia is heavily polluted mainly because of household waste. In 2018, 25.1% of villages in Indonesia experienced water pollution and around 2.7% of the land had contaminated. The accumulation of the garbage that was thrown to the stream leads to several flood disasters. This often happens in Indonesia. The number of flood disasters increases from year by year. Up until 2017, there were 1805 floods occurred and caused 433 fatalities.


 



What will happen if the plastic waste enter the sea?

When plastic waste enter the sea, each of them will split into small particles from 0.3-5 millimeters and often called as microplastic. Microplastic that scattered in the sea will threaten the ecosystem and the live of the organism under the sea, such as coral reefs, fish, and turtle. Organism like fish and turtle prone to accidentally consuming the microplastic. They might not be able to see it and mistake it as plankton or other small fish. In other case, microplastic can also accidentally enter the fish body due to the strong water currents. In addition, if humans consume fish that contaminated with microplastic, it can be dangerous to our health too. According to Indonesian Plastic Industry Association and the Indonesian Statistics Agency (Badan Pusat Statistik) the number of plastic waste in Indonesia has reached 64 million tons per year, where 3.2 million of it was thrown into the sea, so that means there are 85,000 tons of plastic bags in Indonesian sea.





How does the plastic impact the environment? Who will take the responsibility of the plastics waste problems?

The bad habits of throwing away the waste in the river and sea for sure have a real, long lasting impact for the environment, health, diversity of aquatic creatures, social life. Plastic waste is one of the biggest problem in Indonesia. It was a national issue last year, when Indonesia hosted Asian Games in August 2018. The Sentiong River in Jakarta was severely covered with various types of garbage, and mostly were the plastic waste from industrial and household waste. Since it was full of garbage it caused the water to turn into black which is why people often called the river as ‘Kali Item’ or the black river. Moreover there is foul smell emanating from the river, caused by the water evaporating under the sun. The government began to try to solve this problem only because the Asian Games athlete’s guest house was located near the river in Kemayoran Pusat. This might disturb the Asian Games participants who stayed at the athlete's guesthouse, therefore the governments decided to cover the whole river with black nylon mesh.


We are aware that covering the river is not a sustainable solution. Therefore, we need to reduce our plastic waste now. In order to do that, we have to collaborate with government, NGO, manufacturing industry, researcher, and common society. In other word, it is our responsibility now. We can do it in various way, such as: do not litter, reduce the usage of plastic, and start living with zero waste system. I'm ready to start it, how about you?
